How Eliyon thinks in two layers

Under the hood, Eliyon uses a dual-model brain for kids and a paired skin + ear pipeline for infants.

1

Capture a moment

Parent snaps or uploads a photo of a baby, dog, or cat.

2

Emotion analysis

First, Eliyon’s emotion model checks facial cues – crying, laughing, anger, fear, or calm.

3

Health check pipeline

Then, the health model(s) examine skin textures, color shifts, lesions, rashes, or ear canals.

4

Smart decision fusion

If general health seems normal but ear model finds risk, Eliyon prioritizes ear disease (with mild/moderate/severe levels).

5

Human-readable insight

User sees a simple, readable diagnosis like “Mild otitis media” + “Baby looks uncomfortable” with guidance like “Consult pediatrician if symptoms persist.”

FAQ – For judges, doctors, and parents

Transparent, honest, and medically responsible.

No. Eliyon is a decision support + early warning tool, not a doctor. It never prescribes treatment. It simply highlights what looks unusual, so parents know when to seek professional help sooner.

Today, baby health apps and pet health apps are separate, emotion recognition is separate, and ear disease tools usually rely on hardware otoscopes. Eliyon uniquely combines:

  • Multi-species support in one system
  • Emotion + health detection together
  • Skin + ear model fusion with override rules
  • Pure image-based analysis (no extra hardware)

Eliyon can be used by:

  • Parents at home for quick reassurance
  • Pediatric clinics to triage visiting patients
  • Animal shelters and vets for fast checks
  • Rural health workers using a shared smartphone

Because it only needs a camera image, it can scale from a single family to entire public health networks.

Emotion alone is easy to add. What is hard (and unique in Eliyon) is:

  • Emotion models for infants, not adults
  • Emotion linked with specific disease models
  • Fusion logic where, for example, “normal skin + high-risk ear signal” still triggers an alert, even if other models say “fine”.

That multi-model reasoning is what elevates Eliyon beyond a simple “add-on emotion feature”.

In a production version, photos can be processed locally on device or sent to an encrypted server, and then deleted. For the competition demo, you can mention privacy-first design and opt-in data usage only for improving models.
